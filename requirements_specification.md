Executive Summary:  
Our team is creating an app called FindMe Friends. This app is a spin off from Find My iPhone. Instead of sharing location with family and friends, you share your location based upon your interests. You subscribe to an interest (ex. skiing) to see locations of people. There will also be an activities tab, where you can find activities going on near you. Some key features that we have are a chat option, personal bio, Id-based registration for safety, and group size/capacity if you subscribe to an activity going on. Our target audience is everyone, because everyone can bond over interests. We will assume that we will have access to a third party map app in order for our app to operate. The goal of our app is to be safe, user friendly, and a way to branch out. 

Application Context / Environmental Constraints:  
The app will run in Orange County, CA (can be scalable in the future). The app will run on Android and IOS. iPhone 8 and up, Android 10 and up. The app depends on a Wifi or cellular connection. The app interacts with actors such as humans, 3rd party location apps such as Google Maps.

Functional Requirements Analyses: For (at least) five of the suggested optional features and (at least) two of your own ideas), provide a detailed analysis of the pros & cons of that feature for users, including any potential ethical concerns.

Range of a specific person  
Pros: Users won’t have to worry about traveling too far to meet up with friends they meet on the app. Users can feel more comfortable as less people can view their profile if they adjust their range as so. Users can even adjust their range to search for people much farther away that they would likely never interact with. The use of a range also allows users to not be bombarded with an overwhelming amount of information, such as people or social events, which promotes a cleaner and calmer experience with the app.  
Cons: The use of a range could cause certain users to be unable to connect with anyone on the app.The use of a range means that other users could use this range to potentially stalk people. Some users could even be unaware that they have a range, and therefore connect with less people than they would want.  
Ethics: The good thing about a range is that it can prevent other people from accessing information included in a user’s bio. By limiting the amount of people who have access to a user’s information, there will be less people who have ideas of stalking. However, a range can also allow users to determine where people live and the community in which they live. This can result in users meeting less people as the range they set can tell others a lot about their social-economic status.

Real Names  
Pros: By enforcing that users provide their real names we effectively limit digital impersonation. If digital impersonation were to occur, other users would be oblivious to who was actually behind the account leading to numerous safety risks. Additionally, usage of real names are essential to allowing users to form more meaningful and long-lasting connections. A site like discord that allows for fake names works for virtual game lobbies that alternate after every hour. However, for an app that facilitates in person meetings every so often real names are needed for bonding purposes.  
Cons: Privacy concerns for users are a notable con as people may not wish to be displayed to everyone that has the app. Stalkers for example may connect with you over an activity and get all sorts of information by gaining access to your real name. Some people may prefer to go by a different name and not wish to associate themselves with the name on their id.  
Ethics: There are numerous safety concerns with forcing users to share their government name with other users. Stalkers have an easy time finding out information about a person if they are provided with the person’s government name. Additionally, a person’s real name can give clues to a person’s ethnic upbringing, something of which they would not want to be a factor to the people they connect with.

Private & Public setting  
Pros: By going private, users can protect their information from people who may have bad intentions. This would mean that would deter stalkers and other suspicious people from using the app. On the other hand a public profile could allow users to easily connect with others that come from a similar background, such as the same high school, college, or organization. These options allow for more privacy and help cater towards people with varying ideologies on the sharing of their information.  
Cons: Private profiles go against the goal of the app, connecting with those who have shared interests, and thus make it harder for people to find friends. In other cases, private profiles can also be used to stalk others, and make it harder for other users to realize that they are interacting with someone who has bad intentions.  
Ethics: There is little fault with allowing people to hide their information from the public. However, a small issue with private and public profiles is that interactions with private profiles, in some cases could be interactions with people who have committed crimes in the past. Thus, it might be good to check the background of users when they sign up with their id to ensure that private profiles are properly tagged when the person has had issues in the past.

Chat feature  
Pros: The chat feature allows users to easily arrange certain acquaintances from our app to schedule. It also allows its users to build stronger, personal connections before they feel comfortable enough to arrange an in-person meetup. The notifications from such personal interactions also allows an easy reason for users to be reminded of and return to our app, which boosts app retention and engagement.  
Cons: This feature may also enable some of our users with differing intentions (platonic, romantic, or malicious) to steer into unwanted directions and unpleasant experiences. Users may receive spam messages, harassment, inappropriate content, or repeated unwanted contact. Some may also feel pressured to respond quickly or continue conversations that they are no longer interested in. Managing moderation systems for reports and abuse can be costly for the company.  
Ethics: The major concern for this feature is user safety and harassment/stalking prevention. By the nature of our app, we have a responsibility of properly moderating chat messages and providing strong systems of reporting, blocking, and muting capabilities. Privacy is also important, since users expect private conversations to remain secure and not be shared or accessed without permission. Moderation decisions should also be fair, consistent, and unbiased.

Publicly Viewed Bios  
Pros: Allows users to get a mini representation of you as a person and can include potential likes and dislikes so that matching with friends is more compatible. Additionally, it serves as an icebreaker so that when users meet up in the real world the conversation is less awkward and more natural. It also helps users state their goals, such as get in better shape, their availability or their boundaries.  
Cons:Users may share personal and sensitive information such as their workplace, schedule or their exact neighborhood, which causes severe concerns on a location based app. Also, the bio may limit some interactions as users could make judgments before meeting the person based on their bios turning down a potential friendship.Additionally, there would have to be content moderation so that no one is posting inappropriate content on their publicly viewed bio.  
Ethics:There is a safety concern with an app that shares users location and the users having a bio publicly available as it increases the risk of stalking. Bad actors could utilize the app to track a specific person by using their location data and their bio.Additionally, public bios can lead to prejudice or prejudgement as it does reveal personal info users could potentially be discriminated by things said in their bio such as religion, identity, or political views.

Filter (gender, age, etc..)  
Pros:Allows users to adjust their experience to their own comfort level. For example, female users may feel safer and more comfortable linking up with other females on a hike or another activity. Helps users find people that they can relate more to, such as college students meeting up with people in their early 20s, making finding a friend faster and more reliable.  
Cons:The filter can create homogenous relationships that lack diversity, creating an echo chamber for that user, where the same types of people are matched with them. Additionally, depending on the filter, it may be too strict causing users to not see anyone or very few people leading to more churn and less retention. Even with ID-based registration there may be ways to lie about demographics that can affect the accuracy of the filter.  
Ethics: The filters can easily be used to reinforce bias and discrimination amongst users based on these filters. For example, it could foster or strengthen a user's ageism bias if they are only filtering to people of their similar age. In order to address this issue the app itself must have very inclusive filters such as the gender option not being solely male and female but also including transgender or non-binary groups. Forcing people into these filters could exclude potential users.

(Unique) ID-based registration  
Pros: Essential to ensuring a person is who they claim to be. Provides proof of name, birthday, age, and additional information that the user provides to the application.  
Cons: Arising issues are mainly if an app that helps you find and connect with others really needs to have such personal information. Some may argue that the risk that a person fakes their identity is not enough to condone having access to a person’s government official issued document. These types of verifications are more apparent in apps where fake identities can have some catastrophic consequences such as apps with bank account information, or apps where strict age requirements are a must due to explicit content.  
Ethics: The main ethical concern is whether this app can handle or needs to have access to a picture of your government issued id. While digital impersonation is a problem, if a person working on the app were to have malicious intent or if the app was prone to hackers then the users who provided their document will be in some serious trouble.

(Unique) Different tab for recurring group activities  
Pros: A separate tab for recurring group activities allows users to consistently find communities with shared interests, such as weekly hiking groups, study sessions, language exchanges, etc. This can help users build long-term friendships rather than short-lived interactions. Recurring events create familiarity, making it easier for shy or new users to join after seeing the same group multiple times. Organized repeated activities also allows our users to easily configure proper locations for activities that need certain accommodations (courts/study room booking, privacy, etc).  
Cons: This feature may favor already-established groups, making new users feel excluded or intimidated when trying to join. Some recurring groups may become inactive, poorly organized, or misleadingly advertised while still appearing on the app. This would hurt the perceived activeness and organization of our app for new users. There is also a chance that users repeatedly see the same communities, limiting variety over time.  
Ethics: There is an ethical responsibility to ensure recurring groups remain inclusive and do not promote discrimination or bullying. Moderation may be needed if groups become hostile or misuse the platform for scams/harassment. The app should also be transparent about how recurring events are ranked or recommended to other users. Accessibility should also be considered, ensuring that new users and minority groups have equal opportunities to discover and join communities.



Use Cases:  

Range of a specific person  
Basic Flow:  
The user opens the app, and goes into the settings tab  
The user searches around the settings tab for the range setting  
They locate the range in the settings tab, and adjust it to their liking  
The user exits the settings tab and goes to connect with other users  
All the other people the user can connect with are now all within the range that they specified  
The user exits the app  
Alternative Flow:  
The user opens the app and goes into the settings tab  
The user searches around the settings tab for the range setting  
The user locates the range settings and attempts to set their range  
The user isn’t able to get the exact range that they want  
The user settles for a range that is good enough  
The user exits the settings tab  
The user connects with people and now no one they can connect with is beyond the range they set  
The user exits the app  
Exceptional Flow:  
The user opens the app and goes into the settings tab  
The user searches around the settings tab for the range setting  
The user is unable to locate the range setting  
The user continues to search the settings tab and clicks on multiple other settings  
The user quits the app  

Real Names  
Basic Flow:  
The user launches the app and initiates the registration process.  
The user is prompted to fill in their first and last name.  
The user provides a valid first and last name that seems legit.  
The name is stored to be displayed on the profile.  
The user moves onto the next part of registration.  
Alternative Flow:  
The user launches the app and initiates the registration process.  
The user is prompted to fill in their first and last name.  
The user types in a suspicious or incomplete name.  
The system detects the fake name and prompts re-entry while warning the user of the consequences of fake names.  
User re-enters a valid name and proceeds normally  
Exceptional Flow:  
The user launches the app and initiates the registration process.  
The user is prompted to fill in their first and last name.  
The user submits fake/incomplete names numerous times.  
App locks itself explaining the importance of providing your real name and provides technical support if the user has any issues with the process.  
User cannot move on in registration process.  

Private & Public setting  
Basic Flow:  
The user opens up the app  
The user navigates to the settings tab  
The user searches the settings tab for the private and public settings  
The user finds the Private and Public setting  
The user sets it to Private or Public  
The user exits the settings tab  
The user clicks on their own profile  
The user checks to see if their profile is private or public  
The user sees that the correct information is displayed  
The user exits the app  
Alternative Flow:  
The user opens up the app  
The user navigates to the settings tab  
The user searches the setting tab for the private and public settings  
The user finds the Private and Public setting  
The user adjusts the setting  
The user checks their profile to see if the change was properly applied  
The user is unable to tell if their profile has been properly adjusted  
Exceptional Flow:  
The user opens up the app  
The user navigates to the settings tab  
The user searches for the private and public setting  
They are unable to find it  
The user quits the app  

Chat feature  
Basic Flow:  
The user opens the app  
The user navigates to chat tab  
The user selects a person they previously connected with  
The user types a message, asking to arrange an activity or continuing a conversation  
The other user receives the message and replies  
Both users continue the conversation, arranging plans  
The user exits the app  
Alternative Flow:  
The user opens the app  
The user navigates to the chat tab  
The user selects a person they previously connected with  
The user sends a message  
The other user doesn’t respond immediately  
The app notifies the user when a reply is later received  
The user returns to the app and continues conversing  
The user exits the app  
Exceptional Flow:  
The user opens the app  
The user navigates to the chat tab  
The user receives inappropriate/spam messages from an offending user  
The user returns to the chat tab  
The user selects the offender  
The user blocks and reports the offender  
The app confirms the user that the report was submitted, and the offender is no longer able to contact the user  
The user exits the app  

Publicly Viewed Bios  
Basic Flow:  
The user opens the app  
The user navigates to their “Profile” tab from the main dashboard  
The user taps the “Edit Bio” button  
The user types a short description of themselves, including hobbies and interests in the text box provided  
The user taps “Save” button  
The app updates the profile successfully and makes the newly saved bio visible to other users that encounter their profile on the map or the activities tab  
Alternative Flow (Using Prompts):  
The user opens the app  
The user navigates to their “Profile” tab from the main dashboard  
The user taps the “Edit Bio” button  
The user does not know what to type and they need ideas  
The user clicks on the “Bio Prompts” button  
The app provides a list of fill-in the blank icebreakers so that the user can get started  
The user selects one of the provided prompts, fills in their answer, and taps the “Save” button  
The app formats and displays the completed prompt on the user’s public profile  
Exceptional Flow (Content Violation):  
The user opens the app  
The user enters text in their bio that includes inappropriate content such as hate speech, explicit language, or an external link. 
The user taps the “Save” button  
The apps automated moderation scanner detects and flags the inappropriate content  
The system blocks the save action and displays a message similar to the following: “Your bio contains prohibited language or an external link. Please revise”  
The user’s bio is not updated until they remove the flagged content  

Filter (gender, age, etc..)  
Basic Flow:  
The user opens the app  
The user taps the “Filter” tab from the main dashboard  
The app opens the filter menu displaying sliders and toggles for Age, Gender, Distance, etc  
The user adjusts the age slider to have the range of “20-25” and selects the “Women Only” toggle under gender  
The user taps the “Apply Filters” button  
The app refreshes the map, hiding anyone who the does not meet the filter criteria and showing the other users that do  
Alternative Flow (Clearing Filters):  
The user opens the app  
The user previously set strict filters but now wants to see broader ranges of potential friends  
The user taps the “Filter” tab  
Instead of readjusting the sliders and toggles the user simply clicks a “Clear All” button  
The app removes all filters for the user  
The app refreshes the map showing all available friends near the user on the map, not restricting any as all filters are gone  
Exceptional Flow (No Results Found):  
The user opens the app  
The user sets highly restrictive filters such as someone who is exactly 30 years old and is a woman and is within 1 mile  
The user taps the “Apply Filters” button  
The app searches at the database for matching individuals for the filters but finds zero within the user’s area  
The app displays a message that blocks from the filters actually going through, it says something similar to: “No friends found nearby that match these filters, try expanding your filters to see more friends”  
The app provides a convenient “Expand Search Results” button that immediately resolves the issues broadening the users filters for them  

ID-based registration  
Basic Flow:  
User is in the registration process  
User is prompted to upload a government-issued photo ID (driver's license, passport, state ID)  
App displays a message containing information on why government-issued IDs are needed.  
User uploads a clear image of their ID  
App's verification system scans and confirms the ID is valid and matches the name entered during registration  
User is approved and successfully proceeds to the rest of registration  
Alternative Flow:  
User downloads the app and begins the registration process  
User is prompted to upload a government-issued photo ID (driver's license, passport, state ID)  
User uploads an ID that is blurry, cropped, or partially unreadable  
App flags the submission and prompts the user to re-upload a clearer image  
User is given instructions on how to properly photograph their ID  
User re-uploads a valid image and verification proceeds normally  
Exceptional Flow:  
User downloads the app and begins the registration process  
User is prompted to upload a government-issued photo ID (driver's license, passport, state ID)  
The user repeatedly uploads a blurry or fraudulent document, refusing to provide one with the stated name.  
App denies access to full registration and displays a message explaining the ID verification policy  
User is given the option to contact support if they have a legitimate concern such as not owning a government ID or privacy fears  
System flags that account and may suspend it depending on the activity presented.  

Different tab for recurring group activities  
Basic Flow:  
The user opens the app  
The user navigates to recurring activities tab  
The user browses available activities nearby  
The user selects a recurring activity that matches their interest  
The user views the schedule, group, location, etc  
The user joins the activity group  
The app confirms that the user has successfully joined  
The user exits the app  
Alternative Flow:  
The user opens the app  
The user navigates to the recurring activities tab  
The user searches for a specific type of activity such as hiking or study groups  
The user finds multiple results and compares details  
The user selects the activity that best fits them  
The user joins the selected group  
The app confirms the user has successfully joined  
The user exits the app  
Exceptional Flow:  
The user opens the app  
The user navigates to the recurring activities tab  
The user selects the group they are interested in  
The user attempts to join the activity  
The activity has reached capacity  
The app notifies the group is unavailable, and the user does not join the group  
The user returns to browse other recurring activities  
The user exits the app
